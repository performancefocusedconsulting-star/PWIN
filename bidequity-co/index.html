The market reality

The one cost centre nobody measures.

Revenue for UK government strategic suppliers is flat. Competition is intensifying. New contracts are drying up as affordability constraints bite. The strategic imperative is clear: retain the business you have, selectively pursue what you can win, and drive down costs to protect profitability.

Every function in these organisations has been optimised — finance, project delivery, supply chain, HR. Every function except one. Bidding sits outside your ERP, outside your CRM, outside your PMO. It is the one cost centre that determines your future revenue but has never been measured, structured, or governed with the same discipline applied to everything else.

"Major bids are expensive and inconsistent. Firms waste pursuit effort on low-probability opportunities, enter late with weak positioning, and carry high cost-of-sale with uneven governance — eroding margin even when they win."

BidEquity exists to change this. We bring the same rigour to pursuit operations that your organisation already applies to programme delivery, financial management, and operational performance.

The commercial case

No new revenue required.

A FTSE-listed strategic supplier spends £140m per year on bid activity. It generates £270m profit at a 1:3 win rate. Two thirds of that spend produces nothing.

A £40m reduction in annual cost of sale delivers an equivalent profit contribution — without winning a single additional contract. At typical market multiples, that translates to approximately £240m in market capitalisation impact.

No new revenue. No new contracts. Just discipline applied to the cost of winning the ones you already pursue.
