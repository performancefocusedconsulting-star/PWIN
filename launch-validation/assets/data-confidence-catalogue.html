What the data can and cannot tell us

A confidence catalogue for the UK contracts intelligence database. The fact that this exists matters as much as what's on it — it's the credibility-honesty signal underneath every claim PWIN makes.

What we can assert with high confidence

High

That a notice was published, on which date, by which buyer, against which CPV code.

Because: this is what OCDS publishes verbatim. We capture it without interpretation.

High

That an award decision was published, on which date, naming which supplier(s).

Because: same provenance, with multi-supplier awards captured via the lots and award_suppliers junctions.

High

That a contract is approaching expiry, with which incumbent, on which framework.

Because: derived directly from notice end dates, validated by the canonical layer.

What we can assert with medium confidence

Medium

The total value of awards across a buyer or supplier.

Because: framework values record the maximum, not the realised draw-down. Aggregated totals reflect ceilings, not spend.

Medium

That a buyer is part of a consolidated parent (e.g. an executive agency under a Whitehall department).

Because: the canonical buyer layer covers ~85% of notices. The remaining ~15% are eProcurement intermediaries, private contractors publishing under their own name, and a long tail of one-off entities.

Medium

The directors and SIC codes of a UK-registered supplier.

Because: only ~27% of suppliers in the database have a Companies House number on file. The rest are name-only, GB-PPON-only, non-UK, or public-sector bodies.

What we can assert with low confidence

Low

That a sub-organisation (e.g. HMPPS, UKVI, ESFA) has a particular spend profile under its parent department.

Because: about 5% of contract awards (concentrated in MoJ, Home Office, DfE, MoD) are dark at sub-organisation level — the parent name is published, the agency breakout is not. The £25k spend transparency overlay is built but not yet loaded; once loaded, the confidence here improves to medium for those four families.

Low

The historical win rate of a named supplier against a named buyer.

Because: we see published awards, not non-awards. A supplier who bid and lost ten times for one win shows in the data as one win, not 1-of-11. The denominator is hidden.

What we cannot assert

None

Why a particular award decision went a particular way.

Because: scoring rationale is not published. We can infer pattern; we cannot read minds.

None

The bid teams' internal pre-decision win-probability scores.

Because: they are private to each supplier. Our PWIN engine produces a probability score; it does not read the supplier's own.

None

The behaviour of strategic buyers below the FTS publication threshold.

Because: below threshold, awards may publish on Contracts Finder (£10k threshold) or not publish at all. The Contracts Finder ingest is built and live; coverage is improving.
