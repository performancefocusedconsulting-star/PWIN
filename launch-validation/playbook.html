BidEquity / Validation Tour Playbook

The conversation playbook

This document is for me, not for them. They never see it. They see the website pre-read and my face. — Paul

Purpose

Three things I want from every conversation, in priority order:

  1. Validation — does the proposition hold up under honest scrutiny?
  2. Recruitment — does anyone get excited enough to come on board?
  3. Pivot signal — should I redirect this entirely?

The frame is "help me solve a problem here", not "here's what I built, what do you think?".

Discipline. No more than two reserve assets surfaced in any one conversation. A third means I've stopped listening.

Opening & consent (0–4 min)

The framing paragraph (deliver verbatim, then a beat of silence)

"I'm not here to pitch you anything. I've spent a year and a half building something — a methodology and a platform for how strategic suppliers in UK public sector should pursue their bids — and I'm a few weeks away from making it public.

Before I do, I want to spend an hour with people I trust, including you, on three things I genuinely don't know the answer to. I'd value you pushing back hard rather than being kind."

The recording-consent ask

"Before we start — I'd like to record this so I can listen back properly afterwards rather than scribbling notes. The recording stays with me, gets transcribed for my own synthesis, and isn't published anywhere. Is that OK with you?"

If yes: start recording. If no: fall back to written notes via templates/conversation-capture.md. The conversation still happens, no friction.

Substantive overview (4–16 min)

Open deck.html in a second tab. Walk through the eight slides, modulating per audience.

Watch them while talking. If a slide visibly lands or visibly bores them, compress or expand on the fly. The deck is a visual anchor, not a teleprompter.

The four questions (16–55 min)

Pick two or three. Order organically. Silence is welcome.

Q1 What founding-team / leadership configuration would make this platform survive a strategic supplier's "who else is behind this?" question? Listen for: CTO with platform pedigree · recognisable name on the board · partner firm fronting the route to market · names mentioned spontaneously.
Q2 What would actually trigger a top-40 strategic supplier's leadership team to change how they pursue bids? Listen for: competitor-adopts · regulatory shift · revenue/win-rate pressure · board cost-out programme · new commercial leadership · reactions to the "challenger eats their breakfast" hypothesis.
Q3 Who do you know — for either side of this? Listen for: build side (co-founder, director, partner) and buy side (intro to a buyer). Even tentative names. Write them down then.
Q4 What am I missing? If you were me, would you redirect this entirely? Listen for: pivot signals · adjacent markets · adjacent products · adjacent business models.
If they push back on the £3bn / £2bn at slide 2, Q2 has just opened naturally. Skip the rest of the overview and run with it.

Close (55–60 min)

  1. The distilled prompt. "What's the single thing you'd want me to think hardest about between now and when this goes public?"
  2. One exit ask (whichever fits — never plant more than one cold):
    • Introduction. "Who could you put me in front of for this?"
    • Involvement. "Is this something you'd want to be part of in some shape?" (only if the conversation has clearly gone there)
    • Pressure-test loop. "Can I come back to you in three months when this is in the market and tell you what happened?"
  3. A specific thank-you. Name the exact thing they pushed hardest on. Commit to a next step on the spot.
  4. Stop the recording before standing up.

Pre-read emails (send 48–72 hours before)

For audience A — industry insiders

"I've spent 18 months building a methodology and a platform for how strategic suppliers in UK public sector should pursue bids — including a five-year, 175,000-contract intelligence database. Before I take it public next month, I want to spend an hour with people who know how this market actually works. The site is at bidequity.co.uk if you want a 10-minute scan beforehand — particularly the founder interview at /perspective. I'll talk you through what's behind it, and then I want to ask you three or four hard questions I genuinely don't know the answers to. No pitch."

For audience B — senior business non-industry

"I'm a few weeks away from launching a business — BidEquity — that I've been building quietly for the last 18 months. Before I do, I want to spend an hour with people whose judgement I trust, including yours. There are two or three questions I genuinely don't know the answer to that you might help me think through. The website is at bidequity.co.uk if you want a quick look — the founder interview at /perspective is the closest thing to a manifesto. No pitch, no slides, just a conversation."

For audience C — potential commercial buyers

"I'm getting close to launching a business focused on how strategic suppliers like yours pursue UK public sector bids. Before I do, I'm taking an hour with a small number of senior people on the buyer side — not to sell to them, but to ask whether they think the proposition holds up, and what would have to be true for a firm like theirs to entertain it. Worth ten minutes of your time on bidequity.co.uk first if you're willing. I'd value the honest read."

Capture & synthesis

  1. Stop the recording before standing up.
  2. Within 30 minutes: transfer the recording, transcribe (Otter / Notta / MacWhisper / equivalent).
  3. Run the transcript through templates/synthesis-prompt.md.
  4. Save the synthesised Markdown to conversations/YYYY-MM-DD-<initials>-<audience-code>.md (gitignored by default).
  5. Update the row in templates/synthesis-log.csv and synthesis-log.md.
  6. Purge raw audio and transcript once the synthesis has been reviewed.
  7. Read the synthesis log every 3–4 conversations, not after every one.
