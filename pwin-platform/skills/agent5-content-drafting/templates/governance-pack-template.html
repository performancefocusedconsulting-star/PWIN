04 Opportunity & Solution Summary
Opportunity

The Ministry of Defence is re-letting the National Infrastructure Managed Services contract, a 10-year outsource covering facilities management, asset maintenance, and infrastructure lifecycle across 47 sites in the South-West region. The incumbent (Serco) has held the contract since 2016 and is expected to re-bid.

The client has signalled dissatisfaction with the incumbent's technology investment and sustainability performance, creating a genuine competitive opening. Our solution leverages IoT-enabled predictive maintenance and a net-zero transition pathway that directly addresses these gaps.

Solution Approach

Our solution centres on three differentiated propositions: (1) a digital twin platform for real-time asset visibility, reducing reactive maintenance by 35%; (2) a net-zero roadmap achieving carbon neutrality by Year 7; and (3) a social value programme delivering 200+ apprenticeships across the contract term.

The delivery model is hub-and-spoke with a central operations centre in Bristol and satellite teams at each major site. Technology partner integration (Honeywell for BMS, ServiceNow for ITSM) is confirmed with signed ICAs.

Win Themes

1. Predictive infrastructure — technology-led value, not just cost reduction

2. Net-zero pathfinder — first MOD FM contract to guarantee carbon neutrality

3. Regional economic impact — local workforce, apprenticeships, SME supply chain

Key Risks

TUPE cost uncertainty (£2.3m exposure)

Technology partner dependency (Honeywell exclusivity)

Security clearance pipeline (12/14 key staff cleared)
