## Bid Director

### Responsible (R) — Tasks This Role Executes

**SAL-06** — Capture plan finalisation & win strategy lock
- `SAL-06.2.1` Complete Deal Qualification Checklist (DQC) -- attractiveness, deliverability, risk, win likelihood, informed by ITT documentation analysis
- `SAL-06.2.2` Assess strategic fit against sector portfolio and growth plan -- is this the right opportunity for us right now?
- `SAL-06.2.3` Score PWIN against all dimensions -- competitive position, stakeholder relationships, solution readiness, scoring landscape, transition risk, transformational opportunity
- `SAL-06.2.4` Synthesise win strategy -- the integrated narrative tying win themes, scoring strategy, competitive positioning, stakeholder engagement, and transformational approach into a coherent plan
- `SAL-06.3.1` Define the consortium strategy -- prime/sub, JV, SPV, or sole bidder -- with rationale tied to win strategy and competitive positioning
- `SAL-06.3.3` Define the target delivery model across the consortium -- who owns what scope, high-level work breakdown, accountability structure
- `SAL-06.4.2` Prepare formal Bid Mandate for Bid Board approval -- budget, resource, win strategy, consortium, risk, PWIN, go/no-go recommendation
- `SAL-06.4.3` Confirm lock -- capture plan baselined, win strategy locked, consortium strategy locked, methodology gate passed. Feeds GOV-01 (Pursuit Approval)

**SAL-07** — Pre-submission clarification strategy & drafting
- `SAL-07.2.3` Vet and approve clarification register -- Bid Director sign-off before submission to BM-14 for submission management

**SOL-11** — Solution design lock
- `SOL-11.2.2` Baseline and lock solution design pack -- formal sign-off, "Solution Design Complete" milestone fires, feeds GOV-02

**COM-06** — Pricing model finalisation
- `COM-06.2.2` Lock pricing model -- formal sign-off, baseline for GOV-03 (Pricing & Risk Review) and PRD-04 (pricing response documents)

**BM-10** — Storyboard development & sign-off
- `BM-10.2.1` Review and approve storyboard -- Bid Director sign-off, methodology gate passed, drafting can begin

**BM-12** — Lessons learned & knowledge capture
- `BM-12.1.1` Request and attend formal client debrief -- capture scoring, evaluator feedback, and improvement areas

**BM-16** — Win strategy refresh (post-ITT)
- `BM-16.1.1` Refresh win strategy assessment -- has anything changed since SAL-06 that affects our competitive position, win themes, or approach?
- `BM-16.1.2` Refresh PWIN score and confirm continue/stop decision -- formal re-qualification checkpoint

**GOV-01** — Pursuit approval (configurable)
- `GOV-01.1.2` Conduct pursuit approval review -- present to governance panel, debate, capture decision (approve / approve with conditions / reject)

**GOV-02** — Solution & strategy review (configurable)

**GOV-03** — Pricing & risk review (configurable)

**GOV-04** — Executive approval (configurable -- may be multi-tier)

**GOV-05** — Final submission authority (configurable)

- `SAL-03.2.2` Validate competitive positioning with bid team and adjust

**SAL-01** — Customer engagement & intelligence gathering
- `SAL-01.1.1` Gather and review procurement documentation -- ITT notice, prior information notices, market engagement outputs
- `SAL-01.1.2` Review client strategic plans, spending reviews, policy drivers, and published priorities

- `SAL-01.1.4` Map client's existing strategic suppliers, partners, and past procurement activity at a strategic level
- `SAL-01.2.1` Identify client's strategic priorities, pain points, and hot-button issues from available intelligence

**SAL-02** — Incumbent performance review
- `SAL-02.1.1` Review incumbent service delivery against contractual SLAs and performance framework -- met, exceeded, or fallen short
- `SAL-02.1.2` Assess incumbent behaviour, culture, and partnership quality -- does the client like working with them?
- `SAL-02.1.3` Assess incumbent innovation, continuous improvement, and value-add beyond the contract
- `SAL-02.2.1` Assess incumbent price competitiveness -- is the current price point one we can compete against, or must we differentiate elsewhere?
- `SAL-02.2.2` Assess transition stickiness -- TUPE complexity, asset transfer, knowledge transfer burden, client switching cost
- `SAL-02.3.1` Identify transformational opportunities -- where can emerging technology, new delivery models, or fundamentally different approaches disrupt the incumbent's current operating model?
- `SAL-02.3.2` Assess alignment to evaluation framework and client appetite -- will the procurement reward transformational approaches or penalise non-compliance with a prescriptive specification?
- `SAL-02.3.3` Assess our organisational capability to deliver the disruption -- do we have the technology, skills, partners, and track record to credibly propose this?
- `SAL-02.3.4` Model the commercial impact -- does the transformational approach drive a fundamentally different cost base and price point?

**SAL-04** — Win theme development & refinement

- `SAL-04.1.3` Test each theme against competitive positioning -- does this differentiate us from the incumbent and key competitors?
- `SAL-04.1.4` Ensure each theme is evidence-backed -- do we have credentials, case studies, or demonstrable capability to substantiate this claim?
- `SAL-04.2.1` Refine themes against ITT documentation -- align language, scope, and emphasis to the actual requirement and specification
- `SAL-04.2.2` Develop clear messaging per theme -- concise statements that inform solution design, commercial proposition, and storyboard structure
- `SAL-04.2.3` Validate win themes and messaging with bid team -- confirm for capture plan lock

- `SAL-05.1.3` Where client marking scheme exists, analyse granular scoring guidance -- what does a top-scoring response look like per criterion?
- `SAL-05.2.1` Analyse marks concentration -- identify the high-value sections that disproportionately drive the outcome
- `SAL-05.2.2` Conduct score gap analysis -- can we realistically score 100% in each section, and if not, why not, and what is the mitigation?
- `SAL-05.2.3` Develop scoring strategy per section -- what must each response demonstrate to achieve maximum marks, incorporating gap mitigations
- `SAL-05.2.4` Align win themes to evaluation sections -- ensure our differentiators land where the marks are
- `SAL-05.2.5` Confirm scoring strategy with bid team for capture plan lock
