## The Four Value Layers

AI in bid execution creates value at four distinct levels. Each has different economics and a different buyer conversation. Most competitors sell Layer 1 only. PWIN Architect delivers all four.

## Layer 1 — Task Efficiency (bottom-up, computed)

**What it is:** AI reduces the person-days required to execute individual bid activities.

**Computed impact (from AI suitability assessment):**

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Total bid effort (Services archetype) | 505 person-days (methodology baseline) |
| AI days saved | 172.3 person-days |
| Effort reduction | 34.1% |
| Highest-impact workstreams | SAL (43%), PRD (42%), BM (39%) |

**How it works:**
- Document analysis, extraction, and mapping tasks automated (High suitability — 27% of tasks)
- First drafts generated by AI, humans refine (Medium suitability — 47% of tasks)
- Human relationship and governance tasks get preparation support (Low suitability — 26% of tasks)

**Buyer conversation:** "Your bid team spends 34% less effort on a standard bid. That's 172 fewer person-days per pursuit."

**Limitation:** This is the weakest value layer because it understates the real economics. Methodology effort (505 days) represents productive task time, not the actual cost of running a bid team.

## Layer 2 — Timeline Compression (team reservation model)

**What it is:** AI compresses the critical path, which releases the reserved bid team earlier. The real cost of a bid is team size x duration x day rates, not the sum of task effort.

**The reality of large bid costs:**

Bids don't cost by task — they cost by timeline. When a firm commits a bid team to a strategic pursuit:

- 12-15 people are **reserved** for 8-12 weeks
- Those people are costed at their day rate whether today's tasks need them or not
- The Solution Architect isn't costed at "148 days of productive work" — they're costed at "10 weeks on the bid"
- Real bid cost is typically **~2,000 person-days** for a complex services opportunity (4x the methodology baseline of 505)
- The ~1,500 day gap between methodology effort and real cost is reservation overhead: people waiting for upstream inputs, attending reviews, being available for ad-hoc queries, rework cycles

**How AI changes this:**

If AI compresses the critical path by 2-3 weeks on a 10-week bid:
- 2 weeks x 15 people = 150 person-days of reservation cost eliminated
- At blended day rates of £600-800, that's £90k-120k per bid
- Those people become available for the next pursuit 2-3 weeks earlier
- For a portfolio of 10-15 concurrent pursuits per year, the compound effect is significant

**Buyer conversation:** "Your bid team finishes 2-3 weeks earlier. That's not 2 weeks of task savings — it's 2 weeks of a 15-person team's reservation cost eliminated from every bid."

## Layer 3 — Team Fungibility (reduced key-person risk)

**What it is:** AI as institutional memory means team members can be onboarded, offboarded, and shared across bids without the catastrophic knowledge loss that today makes mid-bid team changes a crisis.

**The problem today:**

- Losing a key person mid-bid (Solution Architect leaves, Capture Lead pulled to another pursuit) is the single biggest operational risk on large bids
- New team members require 1-2 weeks to get up to speed by reading email threads, SharePoint folders, and having "context download" meetings with existing team
- This creates extreme dependency on individuals — the Capture Lead's knowledge of client relationships, the Solution Architect's rationale for design decisions, the Commercial Lead's pricing assumptions
- Firms cannot run a person across two concurrent bids because the context-switching cost is too high

**How AI changes this:**

With PWIN Architect capturing the full state of play — decisions, rationale, dependencies, status, strategy, risks:

- **New team member onboarding:** The AI holds the complete bid context. A new Solution Architect reads the AI-maintained state: what's been decided, why, what's outstanding, what the dependencies are. Onboarding drops from 1-2 weeks to 1-2 days.
- **Mid-bid team changes:** Replacing a key person is a managed transition, not a crisis. The institutional memory lives in the platform, not in someone's head.
- **Specialist sharing across bids:** A Commercial Lead can work across 2-3 concurrent bids because the AI maintains context between their sessions. They don't need to rebuild mental state each time they switch.
- **Reduced key-person risk:** The bid doesn't collapse if one person is unavailable for a week. The AI provides continuity.

**Buyer conversation:** "Your specialists can cover more bids simultaneously because the AI holds the context between sessions. You reduce key-person dependency and your talent pool goes further."

**Portfolio multiplier — A-team on every bid:**

The most damaging consequence of team scarcity is talent dilution across concurrent pursuits. The reality:

- A firm has 5-6 genuine A-team specialists: the Solution Architect who wins bids, the Commercial Lead who finds the angle, the Capture Lead with the client relationships
- When 3 strategic pursuits land simultaneously, Pursuit 1 gets the A-team (70% PWIN), Pursuit 2 gets the B-team (45% PWIN), Pursuit 3 gets whoever's available (25% PWIN)
- The firm doesn't lose Pursuit 3 because the methodology was wrong — they lose it because the A-team couldn't be in three places at once
- The cost isn't the bid investment. It's the opportunity cost of the contract not won — potentially £50m-500m ACV

With AI holding institutional memory and providing the intelligence layer:

- A-team specialists operate across all three pursuits simultaneously
- They provide the strategic judgement — the 20% of their time that actually wins bids
- AI handles the 80% that is information assembly, analysis, drafting, and tracking
- A-team insight gets multiplied across the portfolio instead of being locked to one pursuit
- The maths changes from "AI saves cost" to **"AI wins contracts you would otherwise have lost through talent dilution"**

For a firm like Serco with 85 rebid contracts and a finite pool of bid specialists, this is transformational. The same 50 bid professionals could cover 30% more concurrent pursuits — and with A-team quality on all of them, not just the first one through the door.

## Layer 4 — Decision Velocity (informed early gates)

**What it is:** AI brings the point of informed pursuit decisions radically forward — from month 3 to week 1. This is the highest-value layer because it avoids 100% of the cost on pursuits that should never have proceeded.

**The problem today:**

The typical large services bid follows this pattern:

1. **Capture phase (pre-ITT):** Done half-cocked. High-level opportunity assessment, relationship-driven, often optimistic.
2. **ITT receipt:** Large document pack arrives. Bid team assigned.
3. **Weeks 1-4:** Team spends weeks pulling information together, trying to understand:
   - What's the solution? (Not yet clear)
   - What's the pricing strategy? (Not yet modelled)
   - What are the commercial realities of the SLAs? (Not yet analysed)
   - Are there show-stoppers in the legal documentation? (Not yet reviewed)
   - Can we actually win this? (Nobody knows yet)
4. **Go/No-Go gate:** Happens at this point, but the decision is made with incomplete information. Everything always gets through because "we need the bid team to get up to speed before we can have an informed decision."
5. **Weeks 5-8:** More documentation, deeper analysis. A second gate, maybe.
6. **Month 3-4:** Finally enough information exists for a genuinely informed pursuit decision. By this point, 10+ people have been working for months.
7. **Late no-go:** Occasionally, at month 3-4, the decision is made to withdraw. The cost is already sunk.

**The economics of late no-go decisions:**

| Scenario | Cost |
|---|---|
| 10 people reserved for 3 months before informed no-go | ~600 person-days |
| At blended £600-800/day | £360k-480k wasted per pursuit |
| A firm killing 3 of 20 pursuits at month 3 instead of week 2 | £1.1m-1.4m per year in late no-go costs |
| A firm killing 5 of 30 pursuits at month 3 | £1.8m-2.4m per year |

**How AI changes this:**

With PWIN Architect, the first go/no-go gate at week 1 is a fundamentally different conversation. Within days of ITT receipt, the bid team has:

- **Full ITT analysis** — AI has read every document, extracted requirements, mapped evaluation criteria, identified scoring methodology, flagged hurdle marks (SAL-05, SAL-06)
- **Compliance matrix** — AI has mapped every mandatory requirement to response sections, identified compliance gaps (PRD-01)
- **Contract review** — AI has analysed every clause, flagged non-standard terms, identified risk areas and show-stoppers (LEG-01)
- **Competitive landscape** — AI has compiled competitor intelligence, mapped incumbent performance, assessed displacement difficulty (SAL-01, SAL-02, SAL-03)
- **Win strategy draft** — AI has proposed win themes grounded in client values, evaluation criteria alignment, and competitive positioning (SAL-04)
- **Solution feasibility signals** — AI has identified key solution requirements, TUPE implications, technology constraints, delivery risks (SOL-01, SOL-06)
- **Cost envelope** — AI has benchmarked price range from Contracts Finder data, identified commercial model constraints (COM-02)

**This means the Gate 1 conversation becomes:**
- "Here are the 3 show-stoppers in the contract — one requires board-level risk acceptance"
- "The evaluation methodology weights quality at 70% but our evidence library has gaps in 4 of the 8 highest-weighted criteria"
- "The incumbent's SLA performance is strong — displacement requires a transformational proposition we don't currently have"
- "The price envelope is £X-Y based on benchmark data — our cost base can't compete below £Z"

**These are month-3 insights delivered in week 1.** The gate becomes a genuine decision point, not a rubber stamp.

**The value is not in the bids you win faster — it's in the bids you don't pursue.**

| Impact | Calculation |
|---|---|
| Bids killed at week 1 instead of month 3 | 3-5 per year (conservative, for a firm running 20+ pursuits) |
| Person-days saved per early kill | 400-600 (team reservation for 2-3 months avoided) |
| Cost avoided per early kill | £240k-480k |
| **Annual cost avoidance** | **£720k-2.4m** |

This dwarfs the Layer 1 task efficiency saving (172 days x £600 = £103k per bid).

**Buyer conversation:** "How many pursuits did you kill in the last 12 months after investing more than 4 weeks of bid team effort? What did that cost? PWIN Architect gives you the information to make that decision in week 1, not month 3."

## Competitive Differentiation

Most AI bid tools sell Layer 1 only — "AI writes your proposals faster." This is commoditised and low-value.

PWIN Architect's differentiation:
- **Methodology-grounded:** 84 activities, 295 L3 tasks, structured quality criteria, governance gates — not generic AI writing
- **All four layers:** Task efficiency + timeline compression + team fungibility + decision velocity
- **Supervisory intelligence:** 20 AI use cases that watch the bid and tell the bid manager what they're missing (not just respond when asked)
- **Institutional memory:** AI holds the full bid context, enabling team fluidity and knowledge continuity
- **Evidence-based gates:** Gate decisions grounded in AI-analysed data, not subjective confidence

The combination of Layers 3 and 4 is what makes PWIN Architect a platform decision for a client's entire bid operation, not a tool decision for individual proposals.
