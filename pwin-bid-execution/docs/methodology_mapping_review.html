Gap Analysis
Differences between the Bid Lifecycle Playbook and the product activity model. Gaps in both directions — what's in the playbook but not the product, and what's in the product but not the playbook.
Playbook content not in product
These are L1 stages, L3 tasks, or gates from the playbook that have no current product equivalent. Each gap includes a recommendation.
GAP — B1: RFI Submission
RFI Response Management (2 L3 tasks)
Playbook has a formal RFI stage with response planning and drafting. Product assumes ITT/RFP as the starting point.
Recommendation: Consider adding as optional/configurable stage. Some procurement routes start with PQQ/RFI before ITT. Could be a BM activity that's deactivated by default.
GAP — B2: Pre-Proposal Planning / Procurement Process Mapping
Procurement timeline mapping and milestone planning (1 L3 task)
Playbook B2 includes "Map procurement timeline, evaluation stages, and decision milestones." Product captures this in bid setup wizard (submission deadline, procurement route) but doesn't have an explicit activity for mapping the full procurement process.
Recommendation: Fold into BM-01 (Kickoff) or BM-02 (BMP) as an L3 task. The procurement timeline mapping is part of kick-off, not a separate activity.
GAP — B3: Formal Resource Mobilisation Stage
Resource mobilisation as a formal checkpoint (2 L3 tasks + gate)
Playbook has B3 with a "Resource Mobilisation Gate" — full team confirmed, clearances initiated, partners signed. Product has BM-04 (resource tracking) but it's continuous (dur:0), not a gated milestone.
Recommendation: Per your review — add Resource Mobilisation as a formal milestone. Consider splitting BM-04 into initial mobilisation (gated) and ongoing tracking (continuous).
GAP — B4: Win Strategy Refresh post-ITT
Formal re-qualification after ITT receipt (2 L3 tasks + gate)
Playbook B4 has "Win Strategy Re-confirmation" gate — is the win strategy still valid after seeing the full ITT? Product doesn't have this checkpoint.
Recommendation: Proposed as BM-16 in the mapping above. Includes "Bid Board Win Strategy Re-confirmation" gate. Important for large bids where ITT may reveal surprises.
GAP — P5: Content Library Management as explicit workstream
Content library retrieval, adaptation, and gap-filling (2 L3 tasks)
Playbook has P5 as a separate L1 stage. Product distributes this across SOL-10 (evidence strategy) and PRD-03 (evidence assembly).
Recommendation: No new activity needed. Product's split (SOL-10 = strategy, PRD-03 = assembly) is the right decomposition. L3 tasks from playbook P5 are already mapped to those activities.
GAP — B11: Contract Renewal / Re-bid Strategy
Re-bid planning 18–24 months before contract expiry (1 L3 task)
Playbook has B11 as a cycle-restart activity. As you noted — this is the same as "what's our win strategy" whether new or re-bid.
Recommendation: No separate activity needed. The re-bid context is handled by SAL-02 (incumbent review, configurable) and the bid setup wizard's incumbency flag. B11 is the trigger to restart the cycle, not a bid-level activity.
GAP — Procurement Milestones in Gantt
Client-driven procurement timeline events
Playbook B2 captures procurement milestones (ITT release, clarification deadline, site visits, evaluation stages). Product has submission deadline but no formal procurement milestone timeline.
Recommendation: Per point 7 — rename "Governance Gates" to "Key Milestones" with two groups: (1) Procurement milestones (client-driven, from bid setup), (2) Programme milestones (internally driven, from activity completion).
