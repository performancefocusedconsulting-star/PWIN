**Version:** 1.0 | April 2026
**Status:** Draft for review
**Scope:** L2/L3 methodology content changes for the Commercial & Pricing workstream when the bid archetype is Technology/Digital or Consulting/Advisory.
**Reference:** Services archetype mapped in `methodology_gold_standard.md`. This document shows what changes.

## COM-01 — Should-cost Model (Technology/Digital)

### What changes
- Cost model shifts from workforce-dominated (60-80% people in services) to **technology and development-dominated**
- Key cost lines: development team (higher day rates, smaller team), licensing (COTS platforms, cloud hosting), infrastructure, testing environments
- Transition costs become implementation/deployment costs
- No TUPE cost impact — no transferring workforce
- Innovation productivity curve may not apply — technology approach IS the innovation

### L2/L3 Structure

**L2.1: Cost Build-Up**
1. Build delivery team cost model — developers, architects, testers, scrum masters, product managers. Higher day rates but smaller headcount than services
2. Build technology cost model — COTS licensing, cloud hosting, development tools, test environments, infrastructure. This is often the dominant cost line
3. Build implementation cost model — development phases, deployment, data migration, integration testing, go-live support, hypercare
4. Incorporate partner/vendor costs — COTS vendor licensing, specialist subcontractor development

**L2.2: Cost Modelling & Assumptions**
1. Model cost trajectory — development cost front-loaded (build phase), then drops to support/maintenance in run phase. Different profile from services (flat annual cost)
2. Document assumptions — development productivity, technology maturity, scope stability, client readiness
3. Validate should-cost model

## COM-01 — Should-cost Model (Consulting/Advisory)

### What changes
- Cost model is almost entirely **day rates × days**
- Very simple structure — the team IS the cost
- No technology costs (or minimal — analytics tools, travel)
- No TUPE, no transition costs (or minimal mobilisation)
- No innovation productivity curve
- No service credits, no gain share
- Cost model can often fit on a single spreadsheet

### L2/L3 Structure

**L2.1: Cost Build-Up**
1. Build team cost model — day rates per role, days per phase, utilisation assumptions. This is 90%+ of the cost
2. Build non-team costs — travel, expenses, tools/platforms if applicable, management overhead
3. Incorporate associate/subcontractor costs — associate day rates, specialist input days

**L2.2: Cost Modelling & Assumptions**
1. Model cost by phase — if the engagement has phases (discovery, analysis, recommendations, implementation support), cost each phase separately
2. Document assumptions — scope, duration, team utilisation, client availability, travel frequency
3. Validate should-cost model

## COM-02 — Price-to-Win (Consulting/Advisory)

### What changes
- Price-to-win is fundamentally about **day-rate competitiveness**
- Market benchmarks are more standardised — G-Cloud rates, framework rate cards, published government rate ranges
- The question is simpler: "what day rate per grade wins?" not "what TCV wins?"
- Quality/price interaction still applies under MAT — team quality can justify a rate premium

### L2/L3 Structure — same as Services but emphasis shifts:

**L2.1: Market Price Intelligence**
1. Establish day-rate benchmarks — G-Cloud, framework rates, government published rate ranges per grade
2. Assess competitor rate positioning — what are other consultancies charging for similar grades?
3. Assess client rate expectations — do they have a rate card ceiling or framework cap?

**L2.2: Price-to-Win Modelling**
1. Model rate envelope per grade — floor/midpoint/ceiling
2. Compare our cost rates against win rates — is there margin at the competitive day rate?
3. Validate and recommend rate position

## COM-03 — Subcontractor Pricing (Consulting/Advisory)

### What changes
- Partners are **associates**, not subcontractors
- Commercial structure is very simple — associate day rate, availability commitment, expenses policy
- No complex margin stack, gain share, or payment flow design
- L2.1 (inter-party commercial framework) is much lighter — may be a simple associate engagement letter

### L2/L3 Structure

**L2.1: Associate Commercial Framework**
1. Agree day rates and availability with each associate — simple rate negotiation
2. Issue rate confirmation and availability commitment — not a formal pricing request pack

**L2.2: Associate Cost Integration**
1. Integrate associate costs into the team cost model
2. Validate — all associates priced, rates competitive

## COM-04 — Commercial Model & Payment Mechanisms (Technology/Digital)

### What changes
- Payment mechanism shifts from **unitary charge / annual service fee** to **milestone-based payments**
- Fixed price for delivery, not annual service charge
- Payment linked to deliverables and acceptance criteria, not service levels
- No service credits (no ongoing service to penalise)
- No gain share or innovation value sharing (the technology IS the innovation)
- Retention and warranty provisions apply instead of service credits

### L2/L3 Structure

**L2.1: Payment Mechanism & Pricing Structure**
1. Design milestone-based payment mechanism — payments linked to deliverables, acceptance gates, and phase completion
2. Design pricing structure — fixed price, time & materials elements (change requests), support/maintenance pricing post-go-live
3. Design retention and warranty provisions — payment retention until acceptance, defect warranty period

**L2.2: Financial Risk & Commercial Terms**
1. Design change request mechanism — how scope changes are priced and approved (critical for tech delivery)
2. Design acceptance and payment terms — criteria for milestone sign-off, payment triggers, dispute resolution
3. Validate commercial model

## COM-04 — Commercial Model & Payment Mechanisms (Consulting/Advisory)

### What changes
- Payment mechanism is typically **time & materials (T&M)** or **capped T&M**
- Monthly invoicing against timesheets — no milestone payments (unless deliverable-based)
- No service credits, no gain share, no indexation complexity
- May have a cap or budget ceiling with change control for overruns
- Fundamentally simpler — this activity may take 1-2 days not 5

### L2/L3 Structure

**L2.1: Payment Mechanism & Pricing Structure**
1. Design payment mechanism — T&M, capped T&M, fixed price per deliverable, or day-rate with purchase order management
2. Design rate card structure — grades, day rates, overtime/travel policy, expenses

**L2.2: Financial Risk & Commercial Terms**
1. Design scope management and change control — how scope changes are managed within the cap
2. Validate commercial model — simple, client-acceptable, costable
