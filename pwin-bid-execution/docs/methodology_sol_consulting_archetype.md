# SOL Workstream — Consulting / Advisory Archetype

**Version:** 1.0 | April 2026
**Status:** Draft for review
**Scope:** L2/L3 methodology content for the Solution Design workstream when the bid archetype is Consulting / Advisory — professional services, strategic advisory, programme management, or specialist consultancy.
**Reference:** Services archetype mapped in `methodology_gold_standard.md`. This document shows what changes.

---

## Archetype Context

The Consulting / Advisory archetype covers bids where the primary deliverable is professional expertise, advisory services, or programme delivery capability — not an outsourced operational service or a technology product. Examples:

- Strategic consultancy engagement (transformation strategy, organisational design, policy advisory)
- Programme and project management services
- Specialist technical advisory (engineering, environmental, legal, financial)
- Assurance and audit services
- Training and capability development programmes
- Interim management and leadership

**Key differences from Services archetype:**
- The "solution" is the approach, methodology, and team — not an operating model
- There is no TUPE (no operational workforce transfers)
- No service delivery model in the traditional sense — it's an engagement model
- Staffing is the solution — team CVs, skills, and experience ARE the primary evidence
- Innovation is methodology and approach innovation, not technology or operational innovation
- Transition is mobilisation (getting the team in place and productive), not service handover
- Typically shorter duration, lower TCV, team-centric evaluation

## SOL-01 — Scope & Objectives Analysis (Consulting/Advisory)

### What changes from Services
- Requirements are expressed as outcomes, objectives, and deliverables — not service specifications
- The client is typically buying expertise to solve a problem, not a defined service to operate
- Scope boundaries are critical — consulting engagements suffer from scope creep
- Understanding the client's decision-making context matters — who will act on the advisory output?

### L2/L3 Structure

**L2.1: Scope & Objectives Decomposition**
1. Decompose the brief into structured requirements — objectives, deliverables, outcomes, constraints, timeline, governance expectations
2. Identify the problem the client is trying to solve — not just what they've asked for but what they need (same principle as Services but more acute in consulting)
3. Identify scope boundaries and risks — what's in, what's out, where scope creep is most likely

**L2.2: Context & Stakeholder Analysis**
1. Understand the client's decision-making context — who will use the output, what decisions will it inform, what happens after the engagement ends?
2. Assess our alignment to the requirement — do we have the right expertise, methodology, and team for this specific problem?
3. Synthesise scope interpretation document — objectives, deliverables, context, feasibility

### Inputs
- SAL-06: Capture plan (locked)
- SAL-01: Customer intelligence briefing — client strategic context
- SAL-05: Evaluation criteria matrix with scoring approach
- External: ITT documentation — brief, scope of services, deliverables, evaluation criteria

### Outputs
- Scope interpretation document (objectives, deliverables, context, feasibility, scope risks)

### Consumers
- SOL-03, SAL-07, SOL-06

## SOL-02 — Current State & Context Assessment (Consulting/Advisory)

### What changes from Services
- No operating model assessment — there's no service to inherit or transition
- Focus is on understanding the client's current state relevant to the advisory problem
- What has the client already tried? What do they already know? What political context surrounds this work?
- May be minimal or skipped entirely for some engagements

### L2/L3 Structure

**L2.1: Client Context Assessment**
1. Assess the client's current state relevant to the engagement — what exists today in the area we are advising on?
2. Review previous work — has the client or another consultancy already attempted this? What was the outcome? What are the lessons?
3. Understand organisational dynamics and constraints — who are the key stakeholders, what are the politics, what are the blockers to change?

### Inputs
- SAL-01: Customer intelligence briefing
- SAL-10: Stakeholder relationship map & engagement plan
- External: Client documentation — previous reports, strategies, organisational context
- External: Published information on the client's current state

### Outputs
- Current state & context assessment (client context, previous work, organisational dynamics)

### Consumers
- SOL-03, SOL-04

## SOL-03 — Approach & Methodology Design (Consulting/Advisory)

### What changes from Services
- The "solution" is the approach and methodology — how we will tackle the problem
- No operating model, no service lines, no TOM canvas
- The approach must be tailored to this specific engagement — not a generic methodology
- Thought leadership and intellectual property are differentiators — our unique frameworks, models, tools
- The solution positioning decision becomes: proven methodology vs bespoke innovation vs co-creation with client

### L2/L3 Structure

**L2.1: Approach & Methodology Definition**
1. Determine approach positioning — proven methodology (low risk, predictable) vs bespoke/innovative approach vs co-creation with client — with rationale
2. Design the engagement methodology — phases, workstreams, activities, deliverables per phase, decision points
3. Define analytical frameworks and tools — what models, frameworks, or proprietary IP we will apply to this problem

**L2.2: Deliverables & Outcomes Design**
1. Define deliverables — what we will produce, in what format, at what quality standard, at which milestones
2. Define outcomes and success criteria — how the client will measure whether the engagement was successful
3. Validate approach with bid team — is the methodology credible, differentiated, and deliverable by our proposed team?

### Inputs
- SOL-01: Scope interpretation document
- SOL-02: Current state & context assessment
- SAL-06: Capture plan (locked) — win strategy
- SAL-04: Win theme document
- External: Our methodology library, frameworks, IP, and past engagement approaches

### Outputs
- Approach & methodology design (phases, activities, frameworks, deliverables, outcomes, success criteria)

### Consumers
- SOL-04, SOL-06, SOL-07, SOL-11, COM-01, GOV-02

## SOL-04 — Engagement & Delivery Model (Consulting/Advisory)

### What changes from Services
- No service delivery processes — this is about how we work WITH the client
- Engagement model: on-site, remote, hybrid, workshop-based, embedded, independent advisory
- Client co-creation and knowledge transfer are often explicit requirements — the client wants to build their own capability, not just receive a report
- Governance is typically lighter but client involvement is higher

### L2/L3 Structure

**L2.1: Engagement Model Design**
1. Design the engagement model — how we work with the client: on-site/remote/hybrid, embedded/independent, cadence of interaction, client team involvement
2. Design knowledge transfer and capability building approach — how we ensure the client can sustain the outcomes after we leave
3. Design governance and reporting — progress reporting, steering group, quality assurance, milestone sign-off

**L2.2: Delivery Assurance**
1. Define quality assurance mechanisms — peer review of deliverables, client review gates, QA standards for advisory output
2. Validate engagement model — confirm it meets client expectations, is deliverable by the team, is costable

### Inputs
- SOL-03: Approach & methodology design
- SOL-01: Scope interpretation document
- SAL-05: Evaluation criteria matrix with scoring approach
- External: Client expectations for engagement model, working arrangements, knowledge transfer

### Outputs
- Engagement & delivery model (working model, knowledge transfer, governance, QA)

### Consumers
- SOL-06, SOL-07, SOL-11, COM-01

## SOL-05 — DEACTIVATED (Consulting/Advisory)

Technology & digital approach is typically not applicable for consulting engagements. If the advisory engagement requires specific technology tools (e.g., data analytics platforms, modelling tools), these are captured as part of the methodology in SOL-03 rather than as a standalone technology design.

Reactivate if the engagement has a significant technology component (e.g., implementing a tool alongside the advisory).

## SOL-06 — Team Design & Key Personnel (Consulting/Advisory)

### What changes from Services
- **The team IS the solution.** In consulting, the client is buying expertise embodied in specific people.
- CVs are the primary evidence — often the most heavily weighted scoring dimension
- No TUPE — there is no transferring workforce
- Team composition, seniority mix, and named individuals are critical
- Continuity commitments matter — the client wants to know the named people will actually do the work

### L2/L3 Structure

**L2.1: Team Structure & Composition**
1. Design team structure — roles, seniority, reporting lines, client interface points, mapped to the methodology phases
2. Define the seniority and expertise mix — partner, director, manager, analyst — with rationale for this engagement's complexity and profile
3. Design team flexibility — how the team scales across phases, how specialist expertise is brought in for specific workstreams

**L2.2: Key Personnel & CVs**
1. Identify and confirm key personnel — named individuals for every client-facing role with confirmed availability
2. Prepare CVs aligned to evaluation criteria — each CV demonstrates relevance to this specific engagement, not generic career history
3. Define continuity commitment — how we guarantee named personnel will remain on the engagement, substitution policy, client approval process
4. Validate team — confirm capability to deliver the methodology, CVs substantiate our claims, costable for COM-01

### Inputs
- SOL-03: Approach & methodology design
- SOL-04: Engagement & delivery model
- SOL-01: Scope interpretation document
- External: ITT documentation — team requirements, key personnel requirements, CV format requirements
- External: Our workforce — available consultants, skills, experience, availability

### Outputs
- Team design & key personnel (structure, roles, named individuals, CVs, continuity commitment)

### Consumers
- SOL-07, SOL-11, COM-01, SOL-12

## SOL-07 — Mobilisation & Onboarding Plan (Consulting/Advisory)

### What changes from Services
- Much lighter than Services transition — no TUPE, no technology migration, no service handover
- Focus is on getting the team in place, productive, and aligned with the client fast
- Client onboarding — building relationships, understanding the environment, gaining access
- May be as short as 1-2 weeks for a consulting engagement

### L2/L3 Structure

**L2.1: Mobilisation Planning**
1. Design mobilisation approach — team onboarding, client introductions, access and logistics, initial discovery/immersion
2. Plan early deliverables — what we deliver in the first 2-4 weeks to build credibility and momentum (often a discovery report or initial assessment)

**L2.2: Mobilisation Risk & Validation**
1. Identify mobilisation risks — key personnel availability, clearance delays, client readiness, access to information
2. Validate mobilisation plan — achievable, credible, demonstrates we can start delivering value quickly

### Inputs
- SOL-03: Approach & methodology design
- SOL-06: Team design & key personnel
- External: ITT documentation — mobilisation timeline, start date, client site arrangements
- External: Security clearance requirements and processing timelines

### Outputs
- Mobilisation & onboarding plan (team onboarding, early deliverables, risk mitigations)

### Consumers
- SOL-11, SOL-12, COM-01

---

## SOL-08 — DEACTIVATED (Consulting/Advisory)

Innovation & continuous improvement is embedded in the approach and methodology (SOL-03) for consulting engagements. The methodology itself is the innovation — our frameworks, tools, and intellectual property. A separate innovation roadmap is not appropriate for a consulting engagement.

## SOL-09 — Social Value Proposition (Consulting/Advisory)

### What changes from Services
- **Unchanged.** Social value is still mandatory (PPN 06/20, minimum 10% weighting).
- Commitments may skew toward: knowledge transfer, capability building, SME subcontracting, STEM engagement, local employment of junior consultants, pro-bono advisory.
- Same L2/L3 structure as Services archetype.

## SOL-10 — Evidence Strategy & Case Study Identification (Consulting/Advisory)

### What changes from Services
- **Modified emphasis.** Evidence is dominated by:
  - **Key personnel CVs** — often the most heavily weighted evidence. Each CV must demonstrate relevant experience for this specific engagement.
  - **Past engagement case studies** — similar advisory work, similar client domain, similar problem. Outcomes and client testimonials critical.
  - **Methodology credentials** — accreditations, certifications, awards, thought leadership publications
  - **Client references** — named referees from past engagements willing to be contacted
- Same L2/L3 structure as Services archetype but evidence types shift.

---

## SOL-11 — Solution Design Lock (Consulting/Advisory)

### What changes from Services
- **Lighter** — fewer components to consolidate (no operating model, service delivery, technology, TUPE, transition, innovation)
- Core lock components: methodology, engagement model, team with CVs, social value, evidence, risk
- Same L2/L3 structure as Services archetype.

---

## SOL-12 — Solution Risk Identification & Analysis (Consulting/Advisory)

### What changes from Services
- **Lighter** — fewer risk dimensions. Key risks for consulting:
  - Key personnel risk — named individuals become unavailable, client rejects substitutes
  - Scope risk — engagement scope expands beyond what was priced
  - Client dependency risk — client doesn't provide access, information, or decision-making
  - Methodology risk — our approach doesn't work for this specific context
  - Commercials risk — fixed-price advisory with uncertain scope
- No TUPE, technology, transition, or operational workforce risk
- Same L2/L3 structure as Services archetype but risk categories shift.
