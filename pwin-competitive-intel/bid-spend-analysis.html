The Data Gap — From Measured to True

Find a Tender Service captures direct award notices published above procurement thresholds. The majority of strategic supplier activity sits below, behind, or outside this layer — framework call-offs never individually notified, contracts below threshold, defence work redacted by regulation, and subsidiaries not resolved to their parent. Each gap is structural — a feature of UK procurement law, not a data quality problem. FTS alone understates the true win count by an estimated 5×.

The Business Math — Wins to Spend

Bid investment is built from the bottom up, tiered by contract type. The cost anchor is real: a consulting framework call-off with a team of four over two to three weeks costs £30–45k — approximately £3,500 per person-week loaded. Scaling that rate by team size and duration across six contract tiers, and applying estimated pursuit volumes for all 37 strategic suppliers, gives total annual bid investment of ~£1.07bn. Defence mega deal bids cost less than raw team-size arithmetic implies because the pursuit team tapers significantly as the bid matures — peak headcount does not persist for the full 3+ year cycle.

The BidEquity Impact

BidEquity's pursuit intelligence services — Qualify, Verdict, and Win Strategy — enable strategic suppliers to exit unwinnable pursuits earlier and invest bid resource where win probability is highest. The primary lever is pursuit qualification: a reduction in bids committed, not a direct improvement in win rate on pursued work. A 20% reduction in unqualified pursuit is conservative relative to disclosed industry benchmarks for systematic bid/no-bid discipline.

Industry Annual Saving ~£215m
20% fewer unqualified pursuits across ~9,029 annual bids at a blended cost of ~£111k.

Equivalent to eliminating ~1,806 unwinnable bids per year across the strategic supplier portfolio — bids that consume resource, distract teams, and deliver zero return.
Avg P&L Impact Per Supplier ~+8%
£215m across 37 suppliers = ~£5.8m average saving per supplier per year.

On a typical strategic supplier operating profit of £75m (£1.5bn UK government revenue, 5% margin), that is a ~+8% improvement in operating profit — with no revenue change required, no new contract wins, and no headcount reduction.

Key Insight — Win Rate Definitions Are Not Standardised

Serco discloses 32%; Capita discloses 64%. These are not the same metric. Serco measures against all opportunities tracked in pipeline (pipeline conversion). Capita measures against submitted bids only (submitted bid win rate). A supplier with rigorous bid/no-bid discipline could honestly report either number — the choice of denominator masks the real picture. BidEquity's Qualify product operates exactly at this definitional boundary, determining which opportunities progress to submission and directly controlling which metric improves.

Pursuit ROI — Gross Margin Return on Bid Investment

Pursuit ROI = gross margin on revenue won ÷ total pursuit spend. The numerator is order intake TCV multiplied by estimated gross margin (derived from disclosed operating margin plus sector overhead benchmarks). The denominator is modelled from active pipeline size, typical contract values, and blended bid cost — no supplier discloses bid costs in any public filing. The result shows what each pound of bid investment returns in gross margin across the full pursuit portfolio including losses.

The BidEquity Argument — Marginal ROI vs Portfolio Average

A 10–27x Pursuit ROI looks healthy — but it is the mean across all pursuits, including the ~67% that are lost. The marginal ROI on the bottom quintile of pursuits is effectively zero: they consume identical bid resource and return nothing. Removing that 20% raises the effective ROI across the remaining portfolio without a single additional contract win — and frees resource to be reinvested into the highest-probability pursuits where the return is proven.

Methodology & Assumptions
  • Baseline (1,059): Award notices matched across 37 strategic supplier name patterns in bid_intel.db, trailing 12 months to April 2026. FTS / OCDS data. Query reproducible.
  • Framework gap (×3 uplift): UK Government estimates ~80% of central government spend routed via Crown Commercial Service and other frameworks. Call-offs are mini-competitions, not individually notified.
  • Sub-threshold (×0.5): Contracts below £139,688 (central gov goods/services) or £214,904 (sub-central) carry no mandatory FTS publication duty under the Procurement Act 2023.
  • Defence (×0.3): DSPCR 2011 and national security exemptions permit redaction. BAE, Lockheed Martin, MBDA return near-zero FTS records relative to known UK government turnover.
  • Entity gaps (×0.2): Subsidiary trading names, JV vehicles, and name variants not resolved to canonical parent in the current entity layer. KBR = 0 records; Microsoft = 6. Conservative floor.
  • Bid cost anchor: £30–45k for a consulting framework call-off — team of 4, duration 2–3 weeks, validated from practice. Implies ~£3,500/person-week loaded cost (staff + overhead + management). All other tiers scaled from this rate by team size and duration.
  • Tiered bid cost model: Six tiers from call-off (£35k) to defence mega (£17.5m). Defence mega corrected from naive team-size calculation — pursuit teams taper significantly as bids mature; peak headcount does not persist for the full 3+ year cycle. ~2–4 UK competitions/yr × 2–4 prime bidders = ~9 mega bid submissions/yr.
  • Blended bid cost (~£111k): Volume-weighted across all six tiers. Higher than prior £45k estimate which over-weighted small call-offs and under-estimated large-contract costs.
  • BidEquity saving (20%): Conservative estimate of unqualified pursuits exited earlier under systematic qualification discipline. Not a win rate improvement claim. Industry benchmarks support 25–35%.
  • Estimated true wins range: 4,500–8,000+. The 5,295 figure is the midpoint of a wide range. All downstream figures inherit this uncertainty.
  • Not included: Sub-contractor activity (invisible in all reporting), devolved procurement (Scotland, Wales), NHS and local authority call-offs from their own frameworks.
  • Annual report sources (Section 05): Serco FY2025 full-year results (March 2026); Capita FY2025 full-year results; Mitie H1 FY26 results (November 2025); BAE Systems FY2025 results; Babcock FY25 preliminary results (June 2025); Kier FY25 results (September 2025).
  • Gross margin estimates (Section 06): Derived from disclosed operating margins plus sector overhead benchmarks (SG&A 8–12%). Not disclosed directly by any supplier. Pursuit ROI denominator (bid spend) is fully modelled — no supplier reports bid costs in any public filing.
